Bond Pricing & Yield Analysis

A static view of bond pricing analysis with pre-generated graphs and metrics.

This project is a Python-based Bond Pricing & Yield Analysis tool that applies discounted cash flow techniques to price fixed-income securities and explore how bond values change as market yields move.

For this project, I focused on comparing two example coupon bonds:

  • Low Coupon Bond – a bond with a lower annual coupon rate, showing greater sensitivity to changes in yield.
  • High Coupon Bond – a bond with a higher annual coupon rate, allowing comparison of pricing behaviour and interest-rate sensitivity.

The Python code calculates bond prices, Macaulay duration, and modified duration, and generates visualisations including bond price vs yield and bond price vs maturity. These outputs help illustrate the inverse relationship between bond prices and yields, as well as the effect of coupon structure and maturity on interest-rate risk.

This project also demonstrates the ability to apply financial mathematics in a practical setting, combining quantitative analysis with clear visual presentation to support fixed-income valuation and risk assessment.
