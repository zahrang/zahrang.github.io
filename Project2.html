Bond Pricing & Yield Analysis

This project applies financial mathematics to analyse how bond prices change in response to yield and maturity. Using Python, I priced plain vanilla coupon bonds, compared low-coupon and high-coupon bonds, and visualised the inverse relationship between bond prices and yields.

The project also includes duration calculations to illustrate interest rate sensitivity, helping explain why longer-dated bonds and lower-coupon bonds are generally more sensitive to changes in market yields.

Key Features

  • Bond pricing using discounted cash flows
  • Comparison of low-coupon and high-coupon bonds
  • Bond price vs yield visualisation
  • Bond price vs maturity visualisation
  • Macaulay and modified duration calculations

Key Insight

The results show the inverse relationship between bond prices and yields: as yields rise, bond prices fall. They also show that lower-coupon bonds are more sensitive to yield changes than higher-coupon bonds, which is consistent with duration-based interest rate risk.
